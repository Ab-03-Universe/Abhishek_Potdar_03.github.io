About Me

I work in radio astronomy instrumentation and astrophysical data analysis. My research includes building a 3-meter automated radio telescope for observing the 21-cm hydrogen line and studying solar radio bursts associated with solar flares.

Research

Solar Radio Burst Analysis

Statistical study of the temporal association between solar flares and radio bursts (Type II and Type III).

HI 21-cm Galactic Observations

Observation of neutral hydrogen emission at 1420 MHz using a custom-built radio telescope.

Projects

3-Meter Radio Telescope

Development of a complete radio astronomy instrument including dish antenna, feedhorn, LNA, bandpass filter, and SDR receiver.

Antenna & Feedhorn Design

Simulation of dipole feeds, cylindrical waveguide feedhorns, and choke-ring antennas.
